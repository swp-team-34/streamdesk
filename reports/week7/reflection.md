# Week 7 Reflection

## Learning points
- **From finalising the product for MVP v3:** We learned that completing the remaining backlog items – equipment categories/subcategories, kit disassembly guard, location discussions, and design polish – gave the customer a unified, production‑ready tool. Their reaction confirmed that the last week’s efforts directly addressed the real operational needs they described earlier.
- **From the final customer meeting:** The customer’s explicit statement that «what there is now, it is already possible to take and work calmly» showed us that the increment was accepted as a useful, standalone service. Their detailed praise for the speed and focus of the team validated our process and gave us a strong final outcome.
- **From the handover discussion:** We learned that a successful handover includes not only working software but also a clear path for the customer to deploy and start using it independently. The customer immediately planned to roll out the system on their own servers, which indicates they felt confident and equipped to do so.

## Validated assumptions
- **Assumption:** We assumed that the Sprint 5 fixes would bring the product to a state acceptable for independent customer use.
  **Result:** **Confirmed.** The customer confirmed they can start working with the system, will deploy it on their side, and will test warehouse scanning and other features in practice.
- **Assumption:** We assumed that the improved documentation (README, `docs/customer-handover.md`) and the visible GitHub repository would be enough for the customer to proceed with handover.
  **Result:** **Confirmed.** The customer reviewed the updated handover guide and found it clear; they also acknowledged that the repository and team support would be available for follow‑up.
- **Assumption:** We assumed that the kit disassembly guard and the equipment category system were critical for warehouse usability.
  **Result:** **Confirmed.** During the demo, the customer tested the kit behaviour, understood the removal mechanism, and confirmed that it works correctly (“everything is super”).

## Friction and gaps
- **Limited live testing before final acceptance:** While the customer accepted the product, they noted they will need to test the system more deeply (e.g., warehouse scanning, real‑life workflows) after deployment on their own servers. Some adjustments may arise after that testing.
- **Unfinished minor UI polish:** The customer mentioned that some icons were not immediately obvious until explained (“these icons were not for me”), suggesting that a few visual hints could be improved, though they are not blockers.
- **Post‑handover support commitment:** The team agreed to stay in touch and help the customer’s developers if needed. This informal support is not yet structured, which could lead to misunderstandings if many requests come at once.

## Planned response
Given that Sprint 5 is the final sprint and the product has been accepted, we do not plan a formal next Sprint. Instead, we leave the following actions as a post‑course handover and maintenance plan:

1. **Deployment and monitoring support:** Assist the customer with the initial deployment on their servers (this is already planned by the customer). Provide a contact point for urgent issues during the first weeks of use.
2. **Collect and prioritise feedback from real use:** Once the customer begins using the system in production (warehouse scanning, task assignments, location management), we will collect feedback and, if we continue supporting the project, create follow‑up PBIs for critical improvements.
3. **Polish the UI hints for kit management** based on the customer’s comment about icon recognition. If a future release is made, add tooltips or clearer labels for the kit status icons.
4. **Ensure long‑term accessibility of documentation and repository:** The final version of `docs/customer-handover.md`, `README.md`, and the hosted documentation site will remain accessible. The customer and their developers can refer to these for onboarding and troubleshooting.
5. **Finalise the public sanitized demo video and MVP v3 release artifacts** to provide a complete, inspectable record of the finished product for the course evaluation and the customer’s records.
