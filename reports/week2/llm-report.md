# LLM Usage Report

## Tools Used

| Tool | Usage |
| --- | --- |
| Gemini | Transcription of the recorded customer meeting and preparation of an initial text version of the conversation. |
| ChatGPT 5.5 | Assistance with user stories, preparation of the customer meeting script, organization of Markdown report files, and synthesis of the LaTeX report file. |
| Claude Opus 4.8 | Review of repository files for completeness, consistency, clarity, and alignment with the Assignment 2 requirements. |

## AI-Assisted Tasks

- **Customer meeting transcription:** Gemini was used to convert the recorded
  customer conversation into text. The resulting transcript was edited and
  sanitized before publication.
- **User stories:** ChatGPT 5.5 helped formulate user stories in the required
  persona-action-value format and improve their wording. The team determined
  the final stories, stable IDs, requirement statuses, priorities, and proposed
  MVP v1 scope.
- **Customer meeting preparation:** ChatGPT 5.5 helped create a meeting script
  covering recording permission, MIT license consent, user stories, MoSCoW
  priorities, the proposed MVP v1 scope, and the prototype demonstration.
- **Repository documentation:** ChatGPT 5.5 helped prepare the structure and
  initial organization of the Markdown files required for the Week 2 report.
- **LaTeX report preparation:** ChatGPT 5.5 assisted with synthesizing the
  LaTeX source used to prepare the assignment report for submission.
- **Document review:** Claude Opus 4.8 reviewed the prepared files and suggested
  corrections where content was incomplete, inconsistent, unclear, or did not
  follow the assignment requirements.

## Generated Outputs

AI assistance contributed to the preparation or review of:

- [`user-stories.md`](user-stories.md)
- [`customer-meeting-transcript.md`](customer-meeting-transcript.md)
- [`customer-meeting-summary.md`](customer-meeting-summary.md)
- the Week 2 Markdown report structure and index
- the customer meeting script
- the LaTeX source for the Moodle report

The team did not treat generated text as a final source of truth. AI-generated
or AI-edited material was used as a draft and was revised before inclusion in
the assignment.

## Human Review and Validation

- Team members reviewed the transcript against the available meeting recording,
  corrected transcription errors, and removed personally identifiable or
  confidential information.
- User stories and priorities were reviewed by the team and discussed with the
  customer. Final decisions about the requirements and proposed MVP v1 scope
  remained the responsibility of the team and customer.
- The customer meeting script was checked to ensure that the required consent,
  approval, and feedback topics were covered.
- Markdown files were manually checked against the Assignment 2 requirements,
  repository links, and related Week 2 artifacts.
- Claude's review suggestions were evaluated by team members before being
  accepted. Suggestions were not applied automatically.
- The generated LaTeX source was compiled and visually inspected, with
  formatting and content corrections made by the team where necessary.

## Impact and Limitations

The tools reduced the time required for transcription, document organization,
wording, formatting, and requirement checks. They also helped the team identify
missing sections and inconsistencies across related files.

The team recognized that LLM output may contain transcription mistakes,
unsupported assumptions, inaccurate summaries, generic user stories, broken
links, or invalid LaTeX. LLMs also do not have authority to interpret customer
approval or make product-priority decisions. For these reasons, all relevant
outputs required manual review and comparison with the assignment requirements
and customer evidence.

## Responsible Use

No credentials, API keys, private customer information, or other secrets were
intentionally included in prompts or public generated artifacts. The published
meeting transcript was sanitized. AI tools were used for assistance rather
than as autonomous decision makers, and the team remains responsible for the
accuracy, originality, and completeness of the submitted work.
